Article from: i-D

March1997 Issue No.162 P34

by Michelle Stavrinou

"...AudioRom (showcasing the sounds of the band AudioRom) creates a natural and soothing space which moves freely in an infinite bluescape of paralleled abstract shapes. Each one encourages you to explore a whole new interface language, one based on symbols. Each shape you paint or each lyric you help to randomly generate creates a unique track that you can then play with , learning as you explore. This computer-as-toy approach was pioneered by radically different software innovations like Kai's Power Tools and Kumitronics' Feed Your Mind...."
