TOMORROW'S HOMES TODAY - exhibition
http://www.fact.co.uk/exhib.htm#tht

'Tomorrow's Homes Today' is an
exhibition about the idea of the home. It is
about how we live, or might like to live,
and how we use the domestic spaces we
occupy. The exhibition includes new
commissions by a number of artists who
have created environments in which we can
play and dream. Each takes different
aspects of the concept of 'home' and
explores them through sculpture, design
and computer interaction.

AUDIOROM, 'AUDIOROOM'

AudioRom, a collective of artists,
musicians and programmers present an
interactive sound and image installation
that creates the atmosphere of a domestic
games or music room.

JOHN & PAUL BUTLER,
'AKKOMMODATION'

John & Paul Butler take an ironic look at
home design and domestic security in this
large-scale interactive installation which
enables uses to contribute to the work
through a computer controlled
questionnaire.

Co-commissioned by the Foundation for Art
& Creative Technology (FACT) and the
Museum of Science & Industry in
Manchester. Made possible with the
financial support of the National Lottery
through the Arts Council of England.
Supported by Touchscreen Systems Ltd.

JUDITH GODDARD, 'MIRA 09.12'

Judith Goddard has brought the whole
universe into one single room with this
extraordinary projection work exploring
the moment between waking and sleeping.

Co-commissioned by the Foundation for Art
& Creative Technology (FACT) and the
Museum of Science & Industry in
Manchester. Made possible with the
financial support of the National Lottery
through the Arts Council of England.

JENNIFER & KEVIN McCOY, 'SMALL
APPLIANCES'

'Small Appliances' features an antique
stove, a computer and giant projection. It
examines the historical place of women in
the home and our relationship to
technology per se.

The exhibition also includes plans for
future houses and urban dwellings from a
number of leading architects as part of
'Concept House 98' and a short
introduction that looks at how the future
of the home has been imagined in the past.

Jointly commissioned by The Museum of
Science & Industry in Manchester and the
Foundation for Art & Creative Technology
(FACT).
