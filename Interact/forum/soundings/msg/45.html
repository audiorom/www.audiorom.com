Posted by AudioRom Sound Track ISEA 98 on August 29, 1998 at 11:2:32:

Sound Track is an Installation that uses industrial and electronic systems such as trains and computers to explore the notion of physical and digital space.

AudioRom will set up 2 laboratory type computer installation at both Manchester and Liverpool railway stations. The computer installations consist of 2 originally designed interactive interfaces which resembles and represents a combination of a linear rail track and a musical score. Hundreds of iconographic thumb prints designed from industrial and electronic images will act as representations of orchestral and electronic passages which the Audience will be able to audition and then place on the track or score.

All of the interfaces are networked so that the user will see real time responses and interactions from other users / remote users. This also enables the arrangement of the score to be a remote shared experience and also constantly updates the musical / sonic status of the overall arrangement

A small data base programmed from the contents of the train time table connects to a play head / visual representation of the train on the track /score time line. At designated times this representation will move in a linear fashion across the time line according to the position of the train determined by the time table. As the representation of the train collides with the placed iconography, the chosen sounds are triggered and hence performs the composition / arrangement made by the audience.

The performances will be broadcast live via real audio and at the installations on the station. Over the period of the festival it is perceived that the arrangements will develop over time creating a rich sonic composition.

Sound Track
AudioRom
ISEA 98 (International Symposium of Electronic Art)
2 - 7 September

Sound Track takes the work of AudioRom out of the gallery and music arena
and into a site specific public art context. Using the train line between
Liverpool Lime Street and Manchester Piccadilly as its source, Sound Track
looks at the history of the impact of the rail network on society as a
parallel with today's digital network and the electronic revolution. This
is an extension of AudioRom's characteristic fusion of sound and visuals as
the artists continue to place themselves as one of the most innovative
collectives working on this type of synergy. Placing the emphasis firmly on
the sonic in the case of Sound Track, visuals will be drawn from railway
archives and time tabling information in order to create a new form of
musical notation.

The artists will mix archive sound and other sampled pieces with rich
orchestral composition put together with Instrumental. The audience will
be able to make up their own compositions from the visuals which represent
different sounds and sequences, with the train line between Liverpool and
Manchester acting as the linearity of the musical score. Each score will
last approximately 1 hour (the duration of the train journey) and will
be linked to the train timetable.

Compositions can be made at Liverpool Lime Street and Manchester Piccadilly
using purpose built consoles. The audience will then be able to hear some or all
of their composition broadcast via Real Audio and live at the stations. The train
becomes the cursor and is a guide to triggering the sound as it progresses on
its journey.

Sound Track is proof that old and new technologies can co-exist in a
creative context. Digital and analogue are not mutually exclusive and can
be made to interact together just as people interact with the computer
interface. Using the rail network which dates back 150 years, radio, and
the digital combined with traditional and experimental sound, Sound Track
will provide a complex, sumptuous and ultimately hedonistic critique of the
different social revolutions which have taken place over the last two
centuries.

International Symposium on Electronic Art 98

ISEA, the annual International Symposium on Electronic Art is the foremost event of
its kind in the world. isea98, the ninth symposium, has been organised as
a partnership between the Foundation for Art & Creative Technology (FACT),
Liverpool John Moores University and Manchester Metropolitan University.

revolution98 is the largest and most ambitious programme of electronic arts
projects ever staged in Britain. It takes place simultaneously in
Liverpool and Manchester and can be found in galleries, theatres, bars,
clubs, trains and on the internet. Together, the commissions of new work,
exhibitions, performances and events ask just one simple question: How much
has technology changed the rules?

The programme explores the effect of the so-called 'technological
revolution' on art, culture and society through the work of artists,
musicians, computer programmers and others and considers what's become of
the idea of 'REVOLUTION' at the end of the century.

All this is explored, questioned and celebrated in more than 100 diverse
projects by artists from 29 different countries.


For five weeks in September and October the cities of Liverpool and
Manchester play host to one of the most prestigious events in the global
electronic arts calendar. isea98 is both a major international symposium
for experts and activists in the diverse fields of electronic media and a
large-scale exhibition and events programme showcasing some of the most
fascinating and challenging work by artists using electronic and digital
media today.

The theme set by the collaborators of isea98 is 'revolution' and will be
explored critically, culturally and politically through both the six day
symposium and five week exhibition and events programme. Together, the
commissions of new work, exhibitions, performances and events ask just one
simple question: How much has technology changed the rules?

The Foundation for Art & Creative Technology (FACT) is Britain's leading
agency for the commissioning of new work in video and electronic media.
For isea98 it has organised the largest programme of electronic art ever
presented in Britain - revolution98 - which can be found in galleries,
theatres, bars, clubs, trains and on the internet. Ranging from computer
and video-based interactive works, to publicly-sited sound projects,
performances and club events, isea98 will feature world premieres of new
work by acclaimed artists John & Paul Butler (UK), Gina Czarnecki (UK),
Willie Doherty (Ire), Judith Goddard (Uk), Perry Hoberman (US), Kristin
Lucas (US), Andrea Zapp (Ger), Keith Piper (UK), Elizia Volkmann (UK),
amongst many others.

We are delighted to announce Bulgarian artist Nedko Solakov's first
solo-staged exhibition in Britain as part of isea98 along with the first
isea artist's residency awarded to Beijing-based artist Feng Mengbo who
will create new work in Liverpool throughout August.

Specially commissioned sound works and performances are being created by
international sound and vision masters AudioRom (UK), Scanner (UK) and
Granular Synthesis (Austria) for England's leading nightclub Cream,
alongside publicly sited interventions by Cornford & Cross (UK), Nigel
Helyer (Aus) and Mäkelä & Paasonen (Fin).
