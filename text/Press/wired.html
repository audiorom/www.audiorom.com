Future Sound

February 1997, P97

James Cooper

"Not too long ago, AntiRom was leading the field in Rom design by stacking it's discs with cute games that explored the nature of the medium, rather than simply using it as a searchable index or software delivery device...."

"AudioRom is a lot of fun. Slip it into your Mac and you get a screenful of Metalheadzy-style logo overlays, which look totally meaningless. And they are, but click somewhere-anywhere, pretty much and one of 16 mini-music apps will spring up. These are "intuitive" graphical interface thingies that, with a bit of tooling around, allow you to create your own techno and jungle style grooves. They're easy to use and lotsa fun ..."

"It's all very reminiscent of ToshioIwai's "musical insect" interfaces. But then ToshioIwai's doesn't have three deeply cool audio tracks on him as well, does he?"
